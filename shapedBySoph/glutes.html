Some of the Best Glute Exercises

The three muscles that make up the buttocks are the gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, and gluteus minimus. As the name suggests, the maximus is the largest of the three. It is responsible for hip extension, lateral rotation, hip abduction, and even stabilization of that sometimes-painful SI joint. Training your glutes can help with back pain, too!

One thing to note is that since the gluteus maximus works with hip extension, many hamstring exercises are also excellent glute exercises. A deadlift, bridge, or any type of thrust will use both the hamstrings and the gluteus maximus.

Gluteus Maximus Movements Include:

  • Deadlift
  • Sumo Squat
  • Curtsy Lunges
  • Hip Thrusts
  • Glute Bridge
  • Bulgarian Split Squat
  • Glute-ham Raise

The gluteus medius is half the size of the gluteus maximus. It sits more laterally and is worked with lateral rotation, lateral extension, and lateral abduction. This is the muscle that can improve a host of injuries. Knee instability or ankle issues are often traced back to a weak gluteus medius.

Gluteus Medius Movements Include:

  • Frog Pump
  • Lateral Band Walk
  • Side Lunge
  • Cable or Band Hip Extension
  • Fire Hydrant
  • All Single Leg Squat Variations
